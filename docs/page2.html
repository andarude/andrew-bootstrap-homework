Entry the second

God I miss the days when ye could lay anchor in a Spanish port and the monarch he'd say "What be yer name, scallawag?" and ye'd take off yer hat an' hold it to yer chest to better let him see yer face and reply "I 'aven't got one, landlubber, on account of me motharr passed on before she gave one to me" and he'd tell yer he's real damn sorry to hearr that and ask what he can do ye farr and ye'd tell him that ye can't read norr even write neither but yer mighty good with piratin' and can pirate them rival colonial powarrs what ye saw on yer way in and won't ask for nothin' much more than some dubloons and criminal indemnity and he'd keep his queen and princesses inside but send his boy who ain't got married yet even though his motharr tells him he be needin' a woman out with a lantern and some dubloons at night and the two of ye'd get to talkin' and he'd throw yer his grog to take a swig from and watch ye drinkin' from it while he lean'd against the gunwale and when he finally got called back on up to the house he'd take a sip from it too real slow-like like it weren't the rum what he were tryna savor.
